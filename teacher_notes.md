# Teacher Notes — From Seed to Sunflower

Target age: 6–9 years
Duration: 25–40 minutes (reading + activity + quiz + worksheet)
Materials: Computer or tablet for lesson, printer for worksheet, scissors/glue/crayons

## Learning objectives
- Students will be able to list the basic needs of a seed (water, soil, sunlight).
- Students will sequence the main stages of a plant life cycle: seed → sprout → plant → flower → seeds.
- Students will explain the role of pollinators (bees) in flower reproduction.

## Vocabulary
- seed, sprout, root, stem, leaf, flower, pollination, germinate

## Lesson flow
1. Warm-up (3–5 min): Ask students if they have seen seeds or plants grow.
2. Read (5 min): Read aloud the short text on the web page together.
3. Activity 1 (10 min): Sequencing game — students click cards to order the steps. Discuss answers.
4. Activity 2 (5–7 min): Quick quiz on the page.
5. Worksheet (10–15 min): Cut-and-paste ordering, coloring, and short answers.

## Assessment
- Observe sequencing activity and check worksheet answers.
- Use quiz results as quick formative assessment (score displayed to student).

## Differentiation ideas
- Support: Provide picture cards and let students physically order them.
- Challenge: Ask students to write about how a sunflower helps animals (food for birds, seeds for snacks).
- Whole-class: Project the page and do the sequencing together, then let individual students try the quiz.

## Accessibility
- Buttons are keyboard-focusable and include aria attributes.
- Use high-contrast colors and large text for visibility.
- Provide printed worksheets for students who prefer paper.

## Extensions
- Plant real seeds in small cups and observe over days; log growth with drawings or photos.
- Arts: Make a craft sunflower from paper plates and seeds.
- Science: Compare different seeds (sunflower, bean, pea) — which sprout faster?

## Notes for web integration
- Filenames: lesson.html, styles.css, script.js
- Images: The lesson uses a public-domain sunflower image. Replace with local images in your repo if desired.
- To add to the site: place the three web files in a lessons/ or content/ folder and link the lesson page from your site navigation.
